Data Tree

2020

A living interactive digital garden - Bezalel academy of art and design, the Incobator program. Many individuals view the virtual world as a realm that is intangible and metaphysical, serving as a means of escape. However, the truth differs. The maintenance of this world has substantial environmental repercussions and requires a significant amount of resources, which can take a toll on the planet. For instance, the production cycle of a 1-megabyte email uses the same amount of energy as a 60W incandescent bulb that is used for 20 minutes. The "DATA Tree" is a living garden that merges the physical and virtual world to reflect the transparent consumption of digital energy.

How does it work?

The mold contains voxels that can be manipulated using a robotic arm. The chef decides what flavors the user should experience and how they should be blended, and the robotic arm pushes the voxels to the desired position.

The final result shown here exhibits a layered, voxel-like structure with a grid pattern.
